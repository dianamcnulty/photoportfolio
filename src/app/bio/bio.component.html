I can't remember when it all began...

I can't remember a time when cameras and photographs weren't a part of my life. Even as a small child I had a camera. This was back in the film days, when point and shoot cameras where very simple. My parents had given my brother and I an old one to play with. There was never any film in it, and it probably didn't work, but at that age it didn't matter. I was just pretending to take pictures just like mom and dad, but unbeknownst to me, I was learning to see the world around me as I looked through the view finder.

Looking through the lens of a camera changes the way you see the world. You see more than the subject in front of you, you see a mood, you see meaning, or you see a small wonder that has otherwise been ignored. You see fleeting moments in time that will never happen twice, that may never be seen again, except in a photograph.
